The Struggle To Get Borough Park Vaccinated

By Amanda Rozon

Nov 11, 2021

Despite efforts from leading voices in the Rabbinical community and the city to get Borough Park Orthodox Jews protected against COVID-19, the neighborhood remains largely unvaccinated and transmission is high.

Around 48% of people in Borough Park have at least one vaccine dose, well below the city's overall average of 73%, according to COVID-19 data provided by the Health Department. But low vaccination rates aren’t only affecting local positive cases. At least one restaurant in the predominantly Orthodox Jewish neighborhood is feeling the effects of the city’s two-month-old vaccine mandate for indoor dining in New York City’s lowest vaccinated neighborhood.

Data shows the percentage of people vaccinated with at least one dose

This is now the third week in a row that Borough Park has received the highest positive case status. And with the fear of a winter surge just around the corner, the rush to get more Borough Parkers vaccinated is dire.
